# ğŸ“‹ PLU Reference

> [!info] What This Is
> Every piece of produce in a grocery store has a PLU (Price Look-Up) sticker. If you're ever unsure what something is, the PLU tells you exactly what you're holding.

---

## How to Read PLU Codes

| Code Format | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **4 digits** (e.g., 4065) | Conventionally grown | Regular farming practices |
| **5 digits starting with 9** (e.g., 94065) | Organically grown | Certified organic |
| **5 digits starting with 8** (e.g., 84065) | Genetically modified | Rarely used in practice |

> [!tip] Procurement Rule
> **Organic (9xxxx) is nice to have, not essential.**
> 
> If it's available and the price isn't crazy, go for it. If not, conventional (4-digit) is perfectly fine for all AL-ATP-001 protocols.

## Confusion Risk Cross-Reference

Some vegetables look similar. See [[Hazard Identification]] for detailed identification methods.

| Substrate A | Substrate B | Risk Level | Quick Test |
|-------------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Beet | Radish | HIGH | Thumbnail test (radish punctures easily) |
| Swiss Chard | Rhubarb | MODERATE | Chard has edible leaves; rhubarb leaves are toxic |
| Onion | Turnip | MODERATE | Cut in half; onion has layers, turnip is solid |

## Reading Produce Signs

At H-Mart and T&T, produce is typically labeled with:
- English name
- Korean or Chinese name
- Price per unit or per pound/kg

If the PLU sticker is missing or illegible, check the sign above the bin.

> [!tip] When In Doubt
> Staff at Asian grocery stores are generally very helpful. If you're not sure what something is, ask. Nobody will judge you.
