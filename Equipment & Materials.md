## Â§3.3 Measurement & QC Instruments

| Instrument | Lab Designation | Specification | Function |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Measuring Spoons | Volumetric Dispenser | 15mL / 5mL / 2.5mL set | Reagent dosing |
| Measuring Cup | Liquid Measurement Device | mL markings required | Solvent measurement |
| Timer | Chronometer | Phone timer works fine | **CRITICAL** for ATP-C |
| Fork or Chopstick | Stainless Steel Probe | â€” | Poking things to check if done |

> [!danger] Critical Instrument: Chronometer (Timer)
> The **timer is mission-critical** for [[Protocol C - Stovetop-Assisted Wilting|ATP-C]].
> 
> You have exactly 60 seconds of blanching time. Exceeding this results in:
> - Chlorophyll degradation (greens turn olive/brown)
> - Textural failure (mushy instead of tender-crisp)
> 
> **Set a timer. Don't eyeball it.**
> 
> Your phone's timer is perfectly adequate for this purpose.

## Â§3.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

| Equipment | Lab Designation | Hazard Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Oven Mitts | Thermal Protection | Contact burns from handling hot reactors |
| Apron | Splash Guard | Lipid splatter during [[Protocol B - Exothermic Surface Reaction\|ATP-B]] |
| Glasses | Optical Protection | Onion-induced lacrimation (optional) |

> [!tip] Optical Protection Advisory
> When you cut onions, they release syn-propanethial-S-oxide, which is why your eyes water.
> 
> **Mitigation options:**
> - Wear glasses or safety glasses (creates a barrier)
> - Wear contact lenses (also creates a barrierâ€”seriously, this works)
> - Refrigerate onions before cutting (cold reduces volatility)
> - Cut near a vent or open window
> - Accept the tears as part of the process
> 
> You'll figure out what works for you.
